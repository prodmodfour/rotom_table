In a TTRPG, the GM controls what players know. The app enforces this digitally by filtering visible data based on the relationship between the viewer and the combatant.

Own combatants show full data — exact HP, stats, moves, abilities, injuries. Allies show exact HP and injuries. Enemies show only percentage HP. On the tactical grid, own tokens are always visible regardless of [[raw-darkness-penalties-with-presets|fog of war]]; other tokens appear only on revealed cells.

This preserves the tension that makes combat interesting. If players could see an enemy's exact HP, remaining moves, and status details, tactical decisions would collapse into optimization problems. The GM describes what players perceive; the app does the same.
