# UML Business Process

A business process is a coordinated set of activities — parallel or serial — connected to achieve a common goal.

## Key characteristics

- **Sequential and parallel activities** — some must follow a strict order (pack before driving to airport), others happen simultaneously (passenger shops duty-free while luggage loads onto aircraft)
- **Organizational distribution** — activities may occur at different locations with different personnel
- **Interdependency** — all activities connect through pursuit of a common goal

The Workflow Management Coalition defines a business process as "a kind of process in the domain of business organizational structure and policy for the purpose of achieving business objectives."

## Modeling guidance

[[uml]] is suitable for modeling business processes in IT system development projects, but reaches its limits for extensive business process reengineering. Business-process modeling should represent only 5–10% of total project effort.

Most business processes are more understandable than they appear. Apparent complexity typically stems from poor documentation and treatment of functionalities as isolated "islands" rather than comprehensive process chains.

**Reference models** and **comparative analysis** with similar organizations accelerate modeling.
