Creating encounters — selecting species, placing terrain, choosing a significance tier, configuring the grid — requires narrative context that only the GM possesses. Encounter creation stays manual because it is not routine bookkeeping; it is a creative act.

The GM decides what the players face based on story pacing, party composition, location, and dramatic timing. No formula can substitute for "this is the right moment for a hard fight" or "these three Pokemon together tell a story about this habitat." This is why [[automate-routine-bookkeeping]] does not extend to encounter design: automation handles what rules fully determine, and encounter design is not rule-determined.

The app supports encounter creation with tools — budget calculators, significance presets, encounter tables — but the GM drives the process. The tools inform the GM's judgment; they do not replace it.
