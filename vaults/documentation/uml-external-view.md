# UML External View

A [[uml-view|view]] that presents a system as a black box — only goods and services offered are visible; internal mechanics are hidden. Adopted when viewing a [[uml-business-system|business system]] from the perspective of customers, partners, and suppliers.

In [[uml-modeling-it-systems|IT system modeling]], the external view means users care about what the system does, not how it works. Use cases function as "instruction manuals for the user interface."

## Maintaining perspective

Organizations often struggle to maintain the external perspective. Employees familiar with internal transactions find reconstructing the customer view difficult, potentially creating user-unfriendly systems. Unbiased staff or external consultants should contribute to modeling.

The critical rule: never mix external and internal perspectives. Activities initiated by internal employees appear in the [[uml-internal-view|internal view]], not here.
