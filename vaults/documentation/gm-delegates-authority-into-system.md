The GM is the root authority in any tabletop game. Rotom Table's rules are not constraints imposed on the GM from outside — they are the GM's own authority, voluntarily delegated into a system.

This is analogous to a god creating a universe. The act of creation is the dilution of the god's direct control into laws that run without moment-to-moment intervention. The laws of physics don't need the god's approval to execute — they just work. The god intervenes only in exceptional circumstances.

In Rotom Table: the rules execute automatically via [[server-enforcement-with-gm-override]]. Players act freely where rules are deterministic per [[player-autonomy-boundaries]]. The GM intervenes only when something exceptional requires it — an override, not standard operation. New rules enter the system through [[per-conflict-decree-required]], the GM making new laws for the universe.

This is why [[automate-routine-bookkeeping]] works: the system is executing the GM's pre-made agreements, not replacing the GM's judgment. Automation and GM authority are not opposing forces — automation *is* GM authority, running on the GM's behalf.

When deciding where a new mechanic falls, the practical test is: can the rules alone produce the correct outcome? If the rules fully determine it — automate, no human in the loop. If the rules usually determine it but the GM might sometimes want different — automate with override. If the outcome depends on narrative context or situational judgment that only a human at the table possesses — require the GM.
