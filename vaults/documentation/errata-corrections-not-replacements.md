When PTR errata corrects something, it patches the specific affected text — it does not rewrite the surrounding system. The app applies errata surgically: fix the mechanic the errata targets, leave adjacent rules untouched.

This matters because PTR's rules are interconnected. Changing one formula can ripple through damage calculations, stat derivations, or condition interactions. If an erratum says "change the STAB bonus from +2 to +3", the app changes exactly that value and nothing else. It does not re-derive every formula that touches STAB.

The same principle applies to Rotom Table's own corrections: patch the specific problem, verify the fix in isolation, then check whether adjacent mechanics still hold.
