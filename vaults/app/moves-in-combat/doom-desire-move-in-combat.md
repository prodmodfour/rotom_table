Doom Desire appears in the [[encounter-act-modal-move-list]] as a grey (Steel-type) button showing "Doom Desire Steel DB 14 Scene x2". No AC value is displayed in the button.

The [[encounter-move-target-panel]] displays:
- Class: Special, DB: 14
- Range: 10, 1 Target
- SP.ATK: 80
- Effect text: "Doom Desire does nothing on the turn it is used. At the end of the user's next turn, Doom Desire hits, even if the user if no longer on the field. Doom Desire cannot miss."
- A "Damage (DB 14): 4d10+15" line appears with a "Roll Damage" button

Doom Desire has the highest observed DB at 14, producing a massive 4d10+15 damage roll. The move cannot miss, so no AC field appears. The trade-off is the one-turn delay — the move does nothing when used and strikes at the end of the user's next turn. This delayed resolution means the target has a full round to prepare or heal, but the guaranteed hit and extreme damage make it devastating. The effect persists even if the user faints before resolution, making it a strong final act. The effect text contains a typo ("if" instead of "is" in "even if the user if no longer"). Scene x2 frequency allows two uses per encounter.
