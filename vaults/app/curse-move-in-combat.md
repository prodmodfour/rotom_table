Curse appears in the [[encounter-act-modal-move-list]] as a purple (Ghost-type) button showing "Curse Ghost See Text". The "See Text" label replaces the usual frequency display, indicating the move has variable properties.

The [[encounter-move-target-panel]] displays:
- Class: Status, Range: Self
- Effect text: "If the user is not a Ghost Type, Curse has a Frequency of EOT, and when used the user lowers its Speed by -1 Combat Stage, but raises Attack and Defense by +1 Combat Stage each. If the user is a Ghost Type, Curse has a Frequency of Scene, and when used the user loses 1/3 of their Max Hit Points and a target Pokémon or Trainer within 8 meters of the user becomes Cursed. This Hit Point loss cannot be prevented in any way."

Curse is unique among observed moves for having entirely different effects depending on the user's type. Non-Ghost users get a stat-trade buff (EOT frequency) that sacrifices Speed for Attack and Defense — a slow-but-sturdy setup. Ghost users get a costly debuff move (Scene frequency) that inflicts the Cursed condition at the price of 1/3 max HP, with no way to prevent the self-damage. The "See Text" label in the move list and the Self range in the target panel make it impossible to tell which version applies without knowing the user's type. The Move Tester is Normal-type, so it would use the non-Ghost variant here.
