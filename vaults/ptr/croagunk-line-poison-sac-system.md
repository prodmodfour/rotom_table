The [[Croagunk]] line produces [[poison-includes-venom|poison]] through specialized internal sacs. Croagunk stores toxic fluid in expandable orange cheek pouches and in its fingers. Upon evolution, [[Toxicroak]] develops a more sophisticated delivery system: a throat-mounted poison sac pumps toxin through tubes running down the arms directly into the knuckle claws. When Toxicroak croaks, the vibration churns the stored poison to increase its potency.

The resulting toxin is extraordinarily lethal — even a scratch from Toxicroak's claws can be fatal. Despite this lethality, the poison has medicinal applications: when diluted with wild grasses and cooked for two days, it becomes a remedy for lower back pain. Croagunk's finger toxin similarly serves as a pharmaceutical ingredient once properly diluted.
