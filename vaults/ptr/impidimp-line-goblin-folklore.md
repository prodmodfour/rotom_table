Each stage of the [[Impidimp]] >> [[Morgrem]] >> [[Grimmsnarl]] line maps to a progressively more dangerous creature from European goblin folklore. Impidimp embodies the imp or gremlin -- a small, mischievous nuisance that steals and pesters but poses no serious physical threat. Morgrem parallels the redcap, a murderous goblin from English legend that carries a polearm (reflected in Morgrem's blade-shaped hair) and lures victims through deception. Grimmsnarl draws from ogres, trolls, and oni -- massive, brutish humanoids whose strength vastly exceeds their apparent intelligence, though Grimmsnarl retains the cunning of its earlier forms. The biblical parallel to Samson, whose power was bound to his hair, reinforces [[grimmsnarl-hair-muscle-fibers|Grimmsnarl's hair-as-muscle]] concept. This folklore escalation from petty trickster to apex predator is central to the line's identity as Dark/Fairy types representing malevolent fae.
