The [[Lillipup]] line uses its facial fur as a sensory organ. In Lillipup, the long hair around its face functions as a biological radar, detecting subtle changes in air pressure, movement, and environmental conditions. This lets it assess an opponent's strength before committing to a fight — it is brave enough to stand its ground but smart enough to retreat from unwinnable encounters.

As the line evolves, the fur's function shifts from detection to protection. [[Herdier]]'s dense, dark body fur grows continuously and becomes increasingly springy and impervious — claws and fangs that strike it simply bounce off. By [[Stoutland]], the fur has become thick enough to insulate against extreme cold, enabling the species' role as a cold-climate rescue specialist. The same structure serves three purposes across the evolutionary line: sensing, shielding, and insulating.
