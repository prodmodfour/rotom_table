The [[Rattata]] line breeds prolifically. A single mating pair can colonize a new area in short order, establishing nests wherever food is consistently available. This reproductive speed makes them one of the most common Pokemon across nearly every habitat type — caves, forests, grasslands, mountains, and cities.

Their population booms create downstream ecological effects. In Alola, non-native Rattata arrived via cargo ships and reproduced so rapidly they became a public concern, prompting the introduction of [[niche-competition-drives-adaptation|Yungoos as a predator species]] to control them. This is a clear example of how [[sensible-ecosystems]] play out — invasive species disrupt local food webs, and the response (importing a counter-predator) creates new ecological pressures in turn.
