In a three-stage evolutionary line, the first evolution (stage 1 -> stage 2) should have easy [[evolution-trigger-conditions|unlock conditions]]. The second evolution (stage 2 -> stage 3) should be hard.

The first evolution is a coming-of-age moment — the Pokemon proves it's no longer a juvenile. The bar should be low enough that it happens naturally through normal adventuring and training, but [[evolution-easy-not-trivial|not so low that it happens immediately or routinely]]. The second evolution is a transformation into something exceptional. It should demand a significant achievement, a dramatic moment, or sustained dedication.

Two-stage lines fall somewhere in between, calibrated to the species. In all cases, the [[evolution-difficulty-scales-with-bst-jump|jump in Base Stat Total]] between forms is a key input — larger jumps warrant harder conditions.
