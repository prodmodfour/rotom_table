# Fire Generation [X]

## Properties
	- Category: Innate
	- Activation: Active (Swift Action)
	- Energy Cost: 1

## Effect

The user generates fire from innate pyrokinetic energy. X is the trait's potency.

**Generate Fire (Active, Swift Action, 1 Energy):** The user produces a sustained flame on their own body or on a surface in an adjacent square. The flame sheds bright light in an X+1-square radius and dim light for an additional X+1 squares beyond that, and persists until dismissed (free action) or until the user falls unconscious. While burning, a surface delivers searing heat to any creature that touches it (no damage, but painful — the GM may call for a Concentration check). The flame can ignite unattended flammable materials on contact.

The user may maintain up to X flames at a time. Creating a flame beyond this limit automatically dismisses the oldest one.

## Styling

The correct format is `Fire Generation [X]` where X is a number. Do not omit the brackets (e.g. `Fire Generation 2` is incorrect).

## Notes

Fire Generation represents the ability to produce flames from biological processes — internal combustion sacs, friction-generated sparks, or innate pyrokinetic energy. Unlike material creation traits, fire is a process rather than matter: it does not persist independently, but requires fuel or sustaining energy to burn.

This trait pairs naturally with [[fire-manipulation]] — one generates the flame, the other directs it.
