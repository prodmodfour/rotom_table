# Territorial [X] [Y]

## Properties
	- Category: Innate
	- Activation: Passive
	- Energy Cost:

## Effect

The user must claim and defend a territory. When any creature the user's disposition is not Friendly or better toward enters this territory, the user will immediately move to confront and drive out the intruder — attacking if the intruder does not leave.

If the user does not currently have a claimed territory (e.g. while traveling), it will attempt to establish one at the first opportunity — marking boundaries, driving off nearby wildlife, and refusing to leave the area.

This can be suppressed for 1 hour with a DC 10 + X Command, Charm, Persuasion or Intimidate check, modified by the Pokemon's [[pokemon-social-skill-hierarchy|social skill hierarchy]]. If you fail this check, you cannot try again for 1 hour.

## Styling

The correct format is `Territorial [X] [Y]` where X and Y are numbers. Each parameter gets its own brackets. Do not omit the brackets (e.g. `Territorial 2 1` is incorrect).

## Notes

Territorial represents a deep instinct to claim, mark, and defend a home range. Trainers who keep moving may find the compulsion manageable — the Pokemon never stays long enough to fully establish a territory. But trainers who settle in one place will need to manage the Pokemon's aggressive response to anyone it considers an intruder in "its" space.

Y defines the minimum territory size:
- **1** — a small area: a room, a campsite, a single tree, a small pond
- **2** — a moderate area: a clearing, a cave system, a stretch of riverbank
- **3** — a large area: a forest grove, a lake, a hillside
