# Sorcery [X] [Y] [Z]

## Properties
	- Category: Innate
	- Activation: Active (Swift Action)
	- Energy Cost: x * y * z (where x, y, and z are the chosen values for the incantation, up to X, Y, and Z)

## Effect

The user performs a vocalized incantation — a spoken or sung magical chant that produces a supernatural effect. If the user is Silenced or otherwise unable to produce sound, Sorcery cannot be used.

- **X** = Scope (the maximum tier of effect the user can produce)
- **Y** = Range (squares)
- **Z** = Duration (the maximum duration tier the user can sustain)

To perform an incantation, the user chooses a scope tier (x, up to X), a range (y, up to Y), and a duration tier (z, up to Z). The energy cost is x * y * z.

### Scope Tiers

Resisted effects use DC 10 + x. Disposition shifts are resisted by Insight; conditions, mental effects, and barriers are resisted by Focus.

Higher tiers can produce stronger single-target effects, or apply lower-tier effects across multiple targets or an area.

**1 — Cantrip.** No mechanical consequence. Cosmetic changes, phantom sensations, atmospheric shifts — flickering flames, chilled drinks, glowing trinkets. The world notices, but nothing is meaningfully changed.

**2 — Lesser.** One meaningful effect on one creature or object. Modifiers at this tier are ±1.
- *Illusion:* a convincing single-sense illusion
- *Emotion:* shift one creature's disposition by 1 step
- *Warding:* grant +1 to resist a category of effect (a status, damage type, or skill)
- *Curse:* impose -1 to one creature's checks in one skill
- *Divination:* sense the presence and direction of a specific creature, object, or substance within range
- *Transmutation:* alter a superficial property of a small object (color, texture, temperature, smell)

**3 — Greater.** A potent effect on one creature or object. Modifiers increase to ±2. Conditions can be inflicted or removed.
- *Illusion:* a multi-sense illusion, or one convincing enough to provoke physical reactions
- *Emotion:* shift disposition by 2 steps, or inflict/remove Infatuated or Enraged
- *Warding:* grant +2 to resist, or create a barrier (creatures must pass the DC to cross willingly)
- *Curse:* impose -2, or inflict Slowed, Stuck, or Tripped
- *Divination:* learn one specific fact about a creature or object (emotional state, general intent, nature of an enchantment), or enter a sleeping creature's dreams
- *Transmutation:* meaningfully alter an object's property — make it brittle, slippery, sticky, buoyant, or luminous

**4 — Dominion.** A major effect on one target, or a Lesser effect applied broadly (up to 3 creatures or a y × y square area). Stronger conditions become available.
- *Illusion:* a persistent all-senses illusion, or reshape how an area is perceived
- *Emotion:* area-wide disposition shift by 1 step, inflict/remove Confused or Asleep, or trap a sleeping creature in a dreamscape (check repeatable each round)
- *Warding:* anchor a ward to a location protecting all occupants, or seal a passage against a creature type
- *Curse:* anchor a curse to an object or location, or inflict Cursed, Disabled, Vulnerable, or Suppressed
- *Divination:* read surface thoughts of a willing or sleeping creature, or survey an area for all matching creatures/objects
- *Transmutation:* significantly alter material properties, or reshape a small amount of nonliving matter

**5 — Sovereignty.** The full extent of supernatural authority. Any lower-tier effect at its extreme, or effects that reshape reality within the user's domain. Modifiers reach ±3 for area-wide effects. Tier 4 conditions can be inflicted area-wide, and Paralyzed or Frozen can be inflicted on a single target.
- Dreamscape imprisonment with no escape check unless damaged
- Area-wide curse or blessing (±3 to one skill for all creatures)
- Rewrite the emotional landscape of an area (disposition shift by 2 steps for all creatures)
- Create a lasting enchantment on an object that persists independently
- Transmute the fundamental nature of a nonliving object

### Duration Tiers

**1 — Momentary.** One round or a single instant.
**2 — Short.** Up to 10 minutes.
**3 — Extended.** Up to 1 hour.
**4 — Lingering.** Up to 8 hours. Does not require the user's attention after casting.
**5 — Anchored.** Until dispelled or the anchor (object/location) is destroyed.

The user may only maintain one incantation with a duration of Short or longer at a time. Casting a new incantation of Short duration or longer ends the previous one.

### Dispelling

As a Swift Action, the user can attempt to dispel an existing Sorcery effect within Y squares. The user rolls 1d20 + X against DC 10 + X of the Sorcery effect being dispelled. On success, the effect ends immediately. Dispelling costs x + y energy, where x is the dispeller's chosen potency and y is the distance in squares to the effect.

## Styling

The correct format is `Sorcery [X] [Y] [Z]` where X, Y, and Z are numbers. Each parameter gets its own brackets. Do not omit the brackets (e.g. `Sorcery 3 4 3` is incorrect).

## Notes

Incantations are audible. Any creature within Y squares can hear the chanting, even if it is not a target. Sorcery inherently breaks Stealth and Invisibility when used.

Sorcery is not a Move. It cannot be Disrupted by effects that specifically counter Moves, but it can be prevented by anything that prevents vocalization.

A creature that successfully resists an incantation is aware that a supernatural effect was attempted on it but not necessarily by whom.
