# Sound

Sound traits describe how a Pokemon produces controlled sound — whether through vocalization, body resonance, friction, or vibration. They are the biological "hardware" that enables sound-based moves — a move with the Sonic keyword typically requires the user to have a Sound trait.

Most Pokemon can make incidental noise, but a Sound trait means the Pokemon can produce sound deliberately and with enough control or power to use it in battle or communication.

## Sound Traits

- [[vocal]] — controlled vocalization through throat or mouth
- [[singer]] — melodic vocalization; singing, humming, whistling
- [[resonant-body]] — body structure that rings, chimes, or vibrates to produce tones
- [[stridulator]] — sound from friction between body parts
- [[wing-buzz]] — sound from rapid wing vibration

## Styling

When listing a Sound trait on a Pokemon, the correct format is `Sound [Subtype]` where the subtype is one of the entries listed above. Do not omit the brackets (e.g. `Sound Vocal` is incorrect).
