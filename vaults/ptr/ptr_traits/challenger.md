# Challenger [X]

## Properties
	- Category: Innate
	- Activation: Passive
	- Energy Cost:

## Effect

When the user encounters a creature that is visibly larger, stronger, or more imposing than itself, the user will attempt to challenge that creature to combat — approaching, posturing, and escalating to attack if the target does not respond or flee.

This can be suppressed for 1 hour with a DC 10 + X Command, Charm, Persuasion or Intimidate check, modified by the Pokemon's [[pokemon-social-skill-hierarchy|social skill hierarchy]]. If you fail this check, you cannot try again for 1 hour.

## Unlock Conditions


## Styling

The correct format is `Challenger [X]` where X is a number. Do not omit the brackets (e.g. `Challenger 2` is incorrect).

## Notes

Challenger represents a deep competitive drive — the Pokemon is compelled to test itself against anything it perceives as powerful. Unlike [[violent-instincts]], which triggers based on disposition, Challenger triggers based on perceived strength. A Challenger Pokemon may be perfectly friendly toward a creature it wants to fight — it doesn't attack out of hostility, but out of an irresistible need to prove itself. This makes it especially difficult to manage in cities, gyms, or anywhere strong Pokemon are common.
