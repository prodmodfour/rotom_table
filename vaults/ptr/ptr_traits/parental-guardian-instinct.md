# Parental Guardian Instinct [X]

## Properties
	- Category: Innate
	- Activation: Passive
	- Energy Cost:

## Effect

When a young or small Pokemon in the user's care is threatened, the user will immediately and aggressively attack the source of the threat — abandoning any other action, ignoring tactical positioning, and disregarding trainer commands. The user does not distinguish between its own offspring and other small Pokemon it has adopted or is protecting; all are defended equally.

This can be suppressed for 1 hour with a DC 10 + X Command, Charm, Persuasion or Intimidate check, modified by the Pokemon's [[pokemon-social-skill-hierarchy|social skill hierarchy]]. If you fail this check, you cannot try again for 1 hour.

## Notes

[[Leavanny]] will attack anything that threatens young Pokemon in its territory — even if those young are not its own species. Its arm blades, powerful enough to slice through tree trunks, serve both the craft of leaf-cutting and the defense of its charges. This instinct represents the evolutionary shift from the hide-and-endure strategy of [[Sewaddle]] and [[Swadloon]] ([[hiding-instinct]]) to an active, aggressive protector role.

In trainer contexts, this means a Leavanny may break formation to charge a threat to a smaller party member, or refuse to leave a young Pokemon's side during retreat. The protective drive is biological — it cannot be reasoned with, only suppressed.
