# Electricity Generation [X]

## Properties
	- Category: Innate
	- Activation: Active (Swift Action)
	- Energy Cost: 1

## Effect

The user generates electricity from innate bioelectric energy. X is the trait's potency.

**Generate Electricity (Active, Swift Action, 1 Energy):** The user produces a sustained electrical charge on their own body or on a conductive object or surface in an adjacent square. The charge crackles visibly, shedding dim light in an X+1-square radius, and persists until dismissed (free action) or until the user falls unconscious. While charged, a surface or object delivers a mild jolt to any creature that touches it (no damage, but startling — the GM may call for a Concentration check). The user can power or short-circuit simple mechanisms by touching them while charged.

The user may maintain up to X charges at a time. Creating a charge beyond this limit automatically dismisses the oldest one.

## Styling

The correct format is `Electricity Generation [X]` where X is a number. Do not omit the brackets (e.g. `Electricity Generation 2` is incorrect).

## Notes

Electricity Generation represents the ability to produce electrical current from biological processes — bioelectric organs, friction-charged fur, or innate electromagnetic energy. Unlike material creation traits, electricity is energy rather than matter: it does not persist independently, but requires a source or conductor to flow through.

This trait pairs naturally with [[electricity-manipulation]] — one generates the current, the other directs it.
