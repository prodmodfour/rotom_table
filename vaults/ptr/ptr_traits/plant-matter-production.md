# Plant Matter Production [Variable]

## Properties
	- Category: Innate
	- Activation: Passive
	- Energy Cost:

## Effect

The user's body grows and produces plant matter — seeds, leaves, petals, vines, roots, wood, or grass. Variable describes what the Pokemon produces (e.g. Seeds, Leaves, Petals, Vines, Roots, Wood, Grass). A Pokemon may have multiple Variable values if it produces several forms of plant matter.

This trait is the biological hardware that enables plant-producing moves. The Pokemon can grow, shed, launch, or extend its plant matter at will. Outside of combat, this allows the Pokemon to produce its plant matter freely for utility purposes — scattering seeds, growing shade, weaving vines, and so on.

## Moves Enabled

This trait is a prerequisite for moves where the user produces plant matter from their body:

- **Seeds:** [[bullet-seed]], [[seed-bomb]], [[leech-seed]], [[worry-seed]]
- **Leaves:** [[razor-leaf]], [[leaf-blade]], [[leaf-storm]], [[leaf-tornado]], [[magical-leaf]]
- **Petals:** [[petal-blizzard]], [[petal-dance]]
- **Roots:** [[ingrain]]
- **Wood:** [[wood-hammer]]
- **Grass:** [[grassy-terrain]]

## Styling

The correct format is `Plant Matter Production [Variable]` where Variable is a named subtype describing what the Pokemon produces (e.g. Seeds, Leaves, Petals, Vines, Roots, Wood, Grass). Do not omit the brackets (e.g. `Plant Matter Production Seeds` is incorrect).

## Notes

Plant Matter Production describes biological plant growth — the Pokemon's body generates plant tissue as a natural function. This is distinct from [[plant-manipulation]], which controls existing plant matter at a distance through phytokinetic force. A Roselia produces petals (Plant Matter Production [Petals]); a Trevenant controls the forest around it ([[plant-manipulation|Plant Manipulation]]).

Spore and powder production (Spore, Stun Spore, Poison Powder) is covered by [[spore-release]] under [[gas-production]], not by this trait — spores and powders are airborne particulates, not solid plant structures.
