[[technician]] was converted from the PTU ability of the same name.

**PTU original:** Static. Moves with a Damage Base of 6 or lower have their Damage Base increased by +2. Always applies to Double Strike or Fivestrike moves.

**What changed:** The mechanical effect was preserved exactly. The PTR conversion added it as Learnable (in addition to Innate) with unlock conditions, since PTR uses the [[training-dual-check-system]] for learned traits.

**Why Passive with no energy cost:** The PTU version was Static — always on, no action required. This is a permanent optimization of the user's fighting technique, not something toggled on and off. No energy cost because the efficiency *is* the reward of mastering the technique — it doesn't cost extra effort to punch correctly once you've trained it in.

**Why Learnable:** Technician is a skill that can be taught. It's not purely biological hardware — it's trained fighting precision. A Pokemon with the right physical aptitude and mental discipline can learn to optimize its lighter strikes. This also means non-Innate Pokemon can acquire it through [[training-unlocks-traits-and-moves|training]].

**Why these unlock conditions:** The Pokemon Test requires DC 20 Athletics AND DC 20 Acrobatics AND DC 25 Focus ([[unlock-conditions-default-and|all conditions are AND]]). Athletics and Acrobatics represent the physical foundation — you need both strength control and agility to land precise strikes. Focus is the hardest check (DC 25) because the mental discipline to consistently optimize technique under combat pressure is the real bottleneck. The Trainer Test is a low DC 15 — a competent trainer can teach this, the hard part is the Pokemon's own mastery.

**Why the mechanic was preserved as-is:** The +2 DB to moves with DB 6 or lower is elegant and well-tested from PTU play. It rewards multi-hit and low-power move strategies without being exploitable on high-power moves. The Double Strike/Fivestrike exception keeps the trait relevant for its intended niche.
