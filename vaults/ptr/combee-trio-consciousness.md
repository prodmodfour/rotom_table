Each [[Combee]] is composed of three hexagonal segments, and each face possesses its own consciousness with distinct preferences — including different tastes in nectar. Despite this individuality, the trio must coordinate to function: Combee can only fly when the top two faces synchronize their wing flapping. These trios form before birth and remain bonded for life. This composite biology explains why Combee operates as a single creature mechanically while exhibiting subtle internal negotiation about food sources and direction.
