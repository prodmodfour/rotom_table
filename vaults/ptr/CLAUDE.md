# PTR Vault

~1740 atomic notes describing the PTR game system (a fork of PTU 1.05). Notes link to each other with `[[wikilinks]]`. Most notes have a "See also" section pointing to related concepts.

## Starting nodes

These are high-connectivity notes that branch into the major subsystems. Start here when orienting.

- `ptr-vs-ptu-differences.md` — master index of all mechanical changes from PTU to PTR. Best single entry point for understanding the system.
- `trait-definition.md` — what a Trait is (PTR's unified progression mechanic replacing Abilities, Capabilities, and Natures).
- `unlock-conditions.md` — how traits and moves are gated (roleplay, training, level, etc.).
- `action-economy-per-turn.md` — what a combatant can do each turn (Standard, Movement, Swift, Free).
- `effective-stat-formula.md` — how stats become in-combat values (multipliers, combat stages, modifiers).
- `damage-formula-step-order.md` — the nine-step damage calculation pipeline.
- `energy-resource.md` — the Energy/Stamina system that replaced move frequencies.
- `movement-capability-types.md` — the six movement traits (Land, Sky, Water, Phase, Burrow, Teleporter).
- `evolution-trigger-conditions.md` — how evolution is unlocked per species.
- `individual-stats-vs-base-stats.md` — the distinction between species base stats and per-instance stats.
- `sensible-ecosystems.md` — ecological design principles for habitats and encounters.
- `wild-encounter-motivations.md` — why wild encounters happen (not random; always motivated).
- `pokemon-intelligence-scales-with-niche.md` — intelligence follows ecological need.
- `level-up-ordered-steps.md` — the sequence when a Pokemon levels up.

## Subfolders

- `ptr_pokemon_redesigns/` — 95 Pokemon species redesigns (stats, types, traits, move lists). Has its own CLAUDE.md with template rules.
- `ptr_move_redesigns/` — move redesigns with PTR mechanics (energy costs, no frequencies). Has its own CLAUDE.md.
- `ptr_traits/` — custom trait definitions. Has its own CLAUDE.md.
