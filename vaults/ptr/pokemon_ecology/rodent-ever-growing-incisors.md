Rodent Pokemon like [[Rattata]], [[Raticate]], [[Bidoof]], and [[Bibarel]] share a key biological constraint: their incisors grow continuously throughout their lives. If they do not gnaw regularly on hard materials — wood, rocks, cinderblocks, even steel — the teeth overgrow and the Pokemon risks starvation. This compulsion is not a choice but a survival necessity.

This adaptation explains why these species cause property damage in urban and rural settings alike, chewing through door frames, walls, and infrastructure. For [[Raticate]], the teeth are strong enough to gnaw through concrete and steel. For [[Bidoof]], the gnawing extends to trees and rocks near their waterside nests. In a tabletop context, this creates natural hooks for [[wild-encounter-motivations]] — a Rattata infestation gnawing through a building's support beams, or a Bidoof colony undermining a bridge.
