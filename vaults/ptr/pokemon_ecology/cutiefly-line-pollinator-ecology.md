The [[Cutiefly]]-[[Ribombee]] line occupies the pollinator niche in forest and grassland ecosystems. Cutiefly feeds on nectar and pollen through its long proboscis, storing excess pollen in its fur — which transfers to other flowers during foraging. This creates a mutualistic relationship with plant life. The line competes directly with [[Butterfree]] over nectar in flower-rich areas, and Cutiefly is attracted to Gossifleur's pollen and Fomantis's scent. Natural predators include Spinarak and Pineco. The Honey Gather trait shared by both stages reflects this constant nectar-foraging lifestyle.
