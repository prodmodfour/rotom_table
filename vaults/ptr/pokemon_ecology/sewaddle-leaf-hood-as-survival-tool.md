[[Sewaddle]] fashions a leaf hood by chewing leaves and sewing them with silk from its mouth. The hood serves dual purposes: camouflage from predators (folded over its face while sleeping or hiding) and emergency nutrition — when food is scarce, Sewaddle eats its own clothing and produces a replacement later. This self-consuming fallback explains why Sewaddle is a reliable survivor at the bottom of the [[energy-pyramid-rarity|food chain]] despite being small and slow. Sewaddle greet each other by rubbing the sensory knobs on their foreheads together, suggesting the hood does not impair their social awareness.
