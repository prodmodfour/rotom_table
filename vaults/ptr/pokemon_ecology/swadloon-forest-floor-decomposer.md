[[Swadloon]] occupies an ecological niche as a forest floor decomposer. It prefers eating fallen, softened leaves rather than fresh ones, converting them into nutrients through an internal process. Forests where Swadloon live are noted for exceptionally healthy foliage because the nutrients they produce cycle back into the soil and nourish surrounding plant life. This mutualistic relationship with the forest ecosystem — consuming waste and returning fertility — makes Swadloon a keystone species in woodland environments despite its sedentary, defensive posture.
