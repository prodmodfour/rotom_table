[[Heracross]] possesses a pair of thin, membranous flight wings concealed beneath its hardened wing casings (elytra). The elytra are part of the same chitinous exoskeleton described in [[heracross-steel-hard-exoskeleton]], serving as protective covers when the wings are folded. To fly, Heracross lifts the elytra and unfurls its wings, beating them rapidly to achieve surprisingly agile flight despite its bulk.

This is the same mechanism used by real-world rhinoceros beetles and other large Coleoptera — the elytra protect the delicate flight wings at rest, then hinge open for takeoff. The Flier 2 speed reflects slow, labored flight, consistent with a heavy-bodied insect that relies more on ground-based strength than aerial mobility.
