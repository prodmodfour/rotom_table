[[Goldeen]] and [[Seaking]] are herbivorous grazers that feed on aquatic plants, river algae, and submerged vegetation. Their habitat in freshwater rivers and streams provides abundant plant matter along riverbeds and banks. During upstream migrations, they feed on algae growing on rocks and riverbed surfaces. Seaking's autumn fattening — growing plumper and more vibrant before breeding season — comes from intensified grazing on dense aquatic plant growth, not from hunting prey. The horn is a weapon and nest-building tool, not a feeding instrument.
