Both [[Goldeen]] and [[Seaking]] are powerful upstream swimmers with overdeveloped pectoral and dorsal fins that function like muscles. Goldeen maintains a steady five-knot speed against river currents, while Seaking can swim up waterfalls entirely. During spring spawning season, schools of Goldeen swim up falls and rivers in mass migration, and Seaking grow fatter and more vibrant in autumn as they prepare to breed. This exceptional current-fighting ability justifies their high [[swimmer|Swimmer]] speeds (6 for Goldeen, 7 for Seaking) — the jump from 6 to 7 at evolution reflects Seaking's qualitatively superior waterfall-climbing ability.
