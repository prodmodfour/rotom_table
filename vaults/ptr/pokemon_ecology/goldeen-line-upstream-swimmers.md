Both [[Goldeen]] and [[Seaking]] are powerful upstream swimmers with overdeveloped pectoral and dorsal fins that function like muscles. Goldeen maintains a steady five-knot speed against river currents, while Seaking can swim up waterfalls entirely. During spring spawning season, schools of Goldeen swim up falls and rivers in mass migration, and Seaking grow fatter and more vibrant in autumn as they prepare to breed. This exceptional current-fighting ability justifies their Swift Swim trait and high Swimmer speeds (5 for Goldeen, 6 for Seaking).
