When on land, [[Wooper]] coats its body in a slimy, poisonous film that serves two purposes: it prevents the skin from dehydrating outside water and insulates against cold. Touching this membrane bare-handed causes a sharp, shooting pain. This adaptation lets Wooper forage on land despite being primarily aquatic, and explains why it carries both the Poison Point and Water Absorb traits in its [[Wooper|redesign]] — the slime is simultaneously a toxic defense and a moisture-retention system.
