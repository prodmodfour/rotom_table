The [[Shroomish]] → [[Breloom]] evolution is not just a shift from passive to aggressive — it is a shift from instinct to discipline. Shroomish survives by reflex: shake body, scatter spores, wait. Nothing about its life as a [[shroomish-decomposer-ecology|forest-floor decomposer]] requires coordination, timing, or technique. Breloom is the opposite. Its [[breloom-spore-then-strike-combat|spore-then-strike combat]] is compared to professional boxing — footwork, distance management, elastic arm extension with precise timing. The Technician trait mechanically represents this: not raw power, but practiced efficiency.

This means the evolution condition should gate on evidence of trained, deliberate combat behavior — not just "Shroomish hit something" but "Shroomish has developed a fighting discipline." In the wild, this happens through repeated territorial skirmishes or competitive foraging where a Shroomish that only spore-clouds its way through encounters eventually [[niche-competition-drives-adaptation|loses out to species that fight smarter]]. The ones that survive long enough to develop coordinated offensive habits are the ones that evolve. For trained Shroomish, the trainer provides the structure — but the threshold is the same: the Pokemon must demonstrate that it has internalized a combat discipline, not merely reacted to threats.

The [[evolution-difficulty-scales-with-bst-jump|massive BST jump]] (29 → 48) reinforces this — Shroomish is gaining an entirely new body plan and combat role. That kind of transformation should not come from a single lucky hit.
