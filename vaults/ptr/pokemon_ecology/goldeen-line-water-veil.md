[[Goldeen]] and [[Seaking]] produce a thin mucus coating over their scales that keeps their skin perpetually moist and slick. This coating serves two purposes: it reduces drag while swimming upstream, and it insulates the body from extreme temperature — the same mucus layer that lets them glide through frigid mountain streams protects them from [[water-veil|burns]] in combat. The coating is why Goldeen and Seaking feel slippery to the touch and are notoriously difficult to hold onto when caught by hand.
