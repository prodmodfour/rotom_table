The [[Yamask]] line is ecologically tied to ancient tombs, ruins, and desert burial sites. Yamask wanders the ruins of ancient civilizations, possibly seeking traces of its [[yamask-former-human-spirit|past identity]]. [[Cofagrigus]] inhabits tombs and monuments, drawn to places where the living come to pay respects to the dead. The entire line draws from Egyptian funerary culture -- Yamask embodies the ba (the soul's wandering aspect) while Cofagrigus embodies the sarcophagus that houses and protects the dead. Their Desert habitat reflects this tomb-dwelling ecological niche.

Yamask does not eat -- it sustains itself as a [[nullivore]]. When it evolves into Cofagrigus and [[cofagrigus-forgotten-identity|loses its human identity]], it develops a need to feed, becoming a [[psychovore|Psychovore [Reverence]]]. The funerary niche is what makes [[cofagrigus-reverence-feeding|reverence a reliable food source]] -- tombs, pyramids, and sacred ruins are places humans visit specifically to feel awe and respect. Both forms [[cofagrigus-levitation|levitate]] rather than walk -- Yamask as a drifting spirit, Cofagrigus as a hovering monument.
