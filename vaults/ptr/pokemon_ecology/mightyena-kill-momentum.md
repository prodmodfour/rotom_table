When a [[Mightyena]] brings down prey, the kill visibly energizes it. The body language shifts — movements become faster, strikes harder, and the rest of the pack responds to the change. This is the biological basis of [[moxie|Moxie]]: a predatory drive that intensifies through successful combat rather than fading from exhaustion.

This makes sense for an endurance predator. A [[poochyena-line-pursuit-hunting|pursuit hunter]] that tires after its first kill would be poorly adapted to pack life, where multiple targets may need to be brought down in sequence. The surge of aggression after a kill ensures the pack can press its advantage while prey scatters.

[[Poochyena]] lacks this trait — juveniles flee when prey fights back, and their individual tenacity isn't backed by the confidence that comes from successful kills. Only after evolving into Mightyena and integrating into a pack does this kill-momentum become reliable.
