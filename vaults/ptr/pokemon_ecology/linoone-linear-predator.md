[[Linoone]] can sprint at speeds up to 60 mph, but only in perfectly straight lines. It cannot turn while running at full speed and must come to a complete stop before changing direction. When forced to navigate obstacles mid-sprint, it makes sharp right-angle turns rather than curves. This constraint means it frequently overshoots prey that dodges laterally.

The straight-line limitation defines Linoone's entire hunting strategy. It spots prey, aligns its charge, and commits fully to a linear rush. Against aquatic prey in ponds, it bypasses the turning problem entirely by leaping in and snatching targets with its sharp claws. This biomechanical tradeoff — extreme speed for zero maneuverability — is the core of the [[line-charge|Line Charge]] trait and explains why [[Zigzagoon]]'s chaotic movement pattern had to be completely overwritten rather than refined.
