As a juvenile, [[Skorupi]] shares the [[bug-type-accelerated-development|rapid development]] common to Bug-types — growing quickly, molting frequently, and reaching combat readiness faster than non-Bug Pokemon of similar size. Its body prioritizes fast growth over durability, cycling through exoskeleton molts at a pace that keeps trainers visibly aware of its progress.

When Skorupi evolves into [[Drapion]], this changes. Drapion sheds its Bug typing entirely, and with it the accelerated metabolism that drove its rapid juvenile growth. Its body shifts priorities — the energy that once fueled fast molting and quick size gains is redirected into thickening its [[skorupi-line-exoskeleton|armor plating]], refining its [[skorupi-line-venom-precision|venom chemistry]], and sustaining the bulk of a fully realized apex predator. Drapion grows slowly and deliberately, like the Dark-type it has become.

This mirrors real-world arthropod development: many insects grow explosively as larvae, then slow dramatically after their final metamorphosis into adulthood.
