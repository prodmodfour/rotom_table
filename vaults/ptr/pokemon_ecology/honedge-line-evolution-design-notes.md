Design notes for the [[Honedge]] line's evolution conditions.

## The core insight: one intent becomes two

A Honedge is not young — it may be centuries old. It is a [[honedge-spirit-is-killing-intent|warrior's residual killing intent]], fully formed as what it is. What it lacks is not maturity but *complexity*. Evolution for this line is not about growing up. It is about a simple, ancient drive gaining dimensions it never had.

Honedge has one intent: kill. When it finds a quest — a specific creature it vows to destroy — it gains a *second* intent: purpose. Undirected killing intent becomes directed will. That added complexity is what splits one blade into two. The Focus 5 requirement reflects the spiritual discipline needed to hold a vow rather than just lash out.

## Completing the quest creates sovereignty

Doublade completing the quest is the killing intent *achieving its purpose*. But the Combat 6 and Command 6 requirements are what transform a weapon into a sovereign. Combat 6 is total mastery of violence. Command 6 is the ability to lead — something a mere weapon cannot do. A blade that can command has transcended being a blade. That's Aegislash: the [[aegislash-kingmaker-spectral-authority|kingmaker]], a spirit with agency and authority, no longer just hunger shaped like a sword.

## Design constraints we followed

- [[evolution-conditions-must-work-in-wild]] — a wild Honedge can find a rival, vow to kill it, and eventually succeed. No trainer required.
- [[evolution-easy-not-trivial]] — not every Honedge finds a meaningful quest. Many spend centuries as undirected killing intent, never evolving. This explains why Honedge are common and Doublade are not.
- [[evolution-condition-difficulty-scaling]] — the first evolution requires finding purpose (achievable but not automatic). The second requires fulfilling that purpose AND developing leadership (hard, exceptional).
