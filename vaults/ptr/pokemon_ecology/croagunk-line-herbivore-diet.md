The [[Croagunk]] line feeds primarily on marsh vegetation — aquatic plants, algae, and waterlogged grasses found in their freshwater and marsh habitats. Like many real-world frogs that are herbivorous as adults, Croagunk and [[Toxicroak]] graze on plant matter rather than hunting prey for food.

This is distinct from their combat behavior. While the line uses [[croagunk-line-poison-sac-system|poison-coated strikes]] and [[croagunk-dirty-fighting|ambush tactics]] to defend territory and fight threats, they do not hunt for sustenance. The poison system is a weapon, not a feeding tool. Toxicroak's [[toxicroak-dual-temperament|territorial aggression]] is about space control, not predation.

The medicinal use of Toxicroak's toxin — diluted with wild grasses and cooked for two days to treat lower back pain — may reflect the line's own relationship with the plants they consume, as certain marsh grasses could contribute to the toxin's chemical composition.
