The [[Rattata]] line thrives in human environments. They nest in walls, forage through garbage, and infiltrate food stores with ease. Their omnivorous diet and willingness to eat nearly anything means they can sustain themselves wherever humans produce waste. [[Raticate]] extends this adaptability with webbed hind feet that let it swim through sewers and waterways to reach new foraging grounds.

This urban specialization is reflected in the line's Naturewalk (Urban) trait. In Alola, the pressure of introduced predators pushed Rattata populations even deeper into cities, where they became nocturnal to avoid [[niche-competition-drives-adaptation|competition with diurnal Yungoos]]. Alolan Rattata colonies are led by an Alolan Raticate that directs foraging parties — a social structure that emerged specifically from urban survival pressure.
