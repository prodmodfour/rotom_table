The [[buneary_line|Buneary -> Lopunny]] evolution condition is **Know Jump Kick** — a single move gate.

**Jump Kick** marks the moment [[Buneary]]'s combat identity shifts. Young Buneary fight almost exclusively with their [[buneary-ear-coil-mechanism|coiled ears]], using them as spring-loaded bludgeons. But as they grow, their legs lengthen and strengthen, and the most mature individuals begin favoring kicks over ear strikes. Learning Jump Kick is the inflection point — the Buneary has developed enough leg power, coordination, and confidence to commit its whole body to an airborne kick. That commitment is what triggers evolution into [[Lopunny]], whose [[lopunny-kick-based-combat|fighting style]] is built entirely around legs.

**Implicit skill gates:** Jump Kick's own unlock conditions — Natural Weapon: Legs, Athletics 3, Combat 3 — mean a Buneary can't learn it immediately. Buneary starts with Athletics +2 and Combat +1, so real physical development is required before the move becomes available. The evolution condition is one line, but the work behind it is substantial.

**BST calibration:** The BST jump is 36 → 47 (delta 11), moderate for a two-stage line. A single "know move" condition is appropriate per [[evolution-difficulty-scales-with-bst-jump]] — harder than Bidoof's Tech Ed 1 gate in practice (because of the implicit skill requirements), but not as demanding as multi-condition gates used for larger jumps.

**Wild viability:** Wild Buneary develop their legs naturally as they grow. Juvenile Buneary rely on ear strikes because their legs are short and weak relative to their powerful ears. As the body matures, the legs catch up. A wild Buneary that starts landing kicks during territorial scuffles or predator evasion — leaping and striking rather than just fleeing — has crossed the threshold. This happens without a trainer; it's a natural part of physical maturation.

**Friendship parallel:** The canon friendship evolution maps onto this cleanly. A Buneary confident enough to change its fighting style — from defensive ear-coiling to aggressive, committed kicking — has developed the self-assurance that the games represent as friendship. The emotional and physical arcs converge at the same point.
