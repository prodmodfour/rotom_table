Murkrow are deeply embedded in human superstition. Seeing one at night is believed to bring misfortune, giving rise to the folk saying "Get home before the Murkrow fly." Communities have developed protective chants — "Workrum, Workrum — bad luck, don't come" — to ward off the perceived curse. This cultural fear exists because [[Murkrow]] are strictly nocturnal, awakening at dusk, so their presence signals that a traveler has stayed out dangerously late.

The superstition is reinforced by Murkrow's deceptive hunting tactic: when pursued, it lures attackers onto dark mountain trails where they become hopelessly lost. It sometimes does this to travelers purely as a prank, blurring the line between genuine threat and mischief.
