Despite being primarily terrestrial, the [[Skorupi]] line is a competent swimmer. Skorupi's flat, segmented body and multiple legs allow it to paddle through marshland waterways and cross flooded terrain — consistent with its Marsh habitat preference. [[Drapion]]'s larger body and stronger limbs make it an even more capable swimmer. This aquatic affinity connects the line to other arthropod predators that hunt across land-water boundaries, reflected in its Water 3 egg group.
