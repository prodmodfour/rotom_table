[[Seedot]] sustains itself by hanging from tree branches via its stem and drawing moisture directly from the host tree. The more water it absorbs, the glossier and heavier its body becomes -- a visible indicator of nutritional status. When it has drunk enough, it grows too heavy for the branch and drops to the ground with a thump. This parasitic feeding cycle ties Seedot's survival to the health of the forest canopy and explains its Photovore and Geovore traits. It also polishes its body daily using leaves, maintaining the smooth surface that aids its [[seedot-acorn-mimicry|acorn disguise]].
