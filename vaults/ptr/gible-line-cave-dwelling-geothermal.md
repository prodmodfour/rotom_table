The Gible line inhabits caves and volcanic mountain ranges, relying on geothermal heat to survive. [[Gible]] historically lived in the tropics but adapted to temperate climates by digging deep into caverns warmed from below, allowing it to endure even harsh winters. When geothermal heat is insufficient, Gible huddle together for warmth. [[Gabite]] expands these nests aggressively, and [[Garchomp]] ultimately settles in volcanic mountain ranges, returning to its den before its body cools after hunting outside. This dependency on environmental heat explains the line's Cold Intolerance trait and Desert/Cave habitat preferences.
