[[effect-spore]] was converted from the PTU ability of the same name.

**PTU original:** Scene – Free Action. Trigger: hit by a Melee Attack. Roll 1d6: 1–2 Poisoned, 3–4 Paralyzed, 5–6 Asleep.

**What changed:** The Scene frequency was replaced by a 2 energy cost. The trigger, the 1d6 roll, and the three status outcomes were preserved exactly.

**Why energy instead of frequency:** PTR replaces frequency limits with energy costs. A Scene limit means the trait fires once and is forgotten — energy cost means it fires every time, but drains the user's stamina. This makes the trait feel like a real biological response rather than an arbitrary once-per-encounter toggle. A Shroomish that keeps getting punched keeps releasing spores, but eventually exhausts itself.

**Why involuntary:** The PTU version was a Free Action, implying player choice. But the lore is clear — [[shroomish-spore-defense-mechanism|Shroomish scatters spores reflexively when struck]]. The spore structures rupture on impact. Making it involuntary means the Pokemon can't choose to suppress it, which matches the biology and also means the energy cost is a genuine risk — getting hit repeatedly in melee will drain stamina whether the user wants it to or not.

**Why the mechanic was preserved as-is:** The 1d6 random status is simple, fast at the table, and reflects the unpredictable mix of spore types released under duress. No reason to change what works.

**Structural precedent:** [[bitter-oil]] uses the same template — Innate, Involuntary Trigger, 2 energy, melee hit trigger. Effect Spore is the spore-based version of the same defensive reflex pattern.
