"Easy" evolution conditions must still represent a meaningful threshold, not something that happens routinely in the species' daily life. If a Honedge kills prey constantly, then "kill something" would mean every wild Honedge evolves almost immediately — making first-stage specimens ecologically rare. But we see first-stage Pokemon in the wild all the time, so the condition must be something that takes time or circumstance to encounter.

Easy means *achievable through normal life without extraordinary effort*, not *inevitable on day one*. The condition should be a coming-of-age gate: something that most specimens eventually achieve, but not immediately or automatically.
