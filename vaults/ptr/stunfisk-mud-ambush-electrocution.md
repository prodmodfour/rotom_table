[[Stunfisk]] buries itself in coastal mudflats and waits for prey to step on or brush against its body, then delivers an electric shock. Its electricity-generating organs evolved through symbiosis with bacteria in the mud it inhabits. The species' skin is tough enough that even a sumo wrestler stepping on it causes no harm — the durability is purely defensive, letting Stunfisk endure being trodden on until it can discharge. If the shock fails to paralyze, Stunfisk contorts itself with surprising speed and flops away. This trap-and-shock hunting style explains its Stealth (+4), Static trait, and Tremorsense — it detects prey by vibration through the substrate, not sight.
