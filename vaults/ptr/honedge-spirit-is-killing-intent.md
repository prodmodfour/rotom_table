The spirit that inhabits a [[Honedge]] is not the ghost of a dead warrior. It is the warrior's residual killing intent — the desire to fight and kill that outlived the person. When a warrior dies but their drive to battle persists, that psychic residue bonds to their weapon and animates it. The blade remembers nothing of who held it, only the urge to cut.

A Honedge is not young or juvenile. The killing intent it carries may be hundreds of years old — the residue of a soldier from a forgotten war, still burning in steel. It is ancient, potent, and fully formed as what it is. What it lacks is not maturity but *complexity*. It has one drive and no capacity beyond it.
