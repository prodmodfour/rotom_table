A Pokemon is at least as intelligent as it needs to be to survive in its natural habitat. Predator species can distinguish between safe prey (Rattata) and dangerous targets (Nidoran) to avoid being poisoned. This adaptive intelligence means wild Pokemon make tactically sensible decisions during encounters. The [[lillipup-line-sensory-fur|Lillipup line]] demonstrates this well — Lillipup uses its facial fur to assess opponent strength and avoids fights it cannot win, a clear case of intelligence matching ecological need.

This principle shapes how GMs should roleplay wild Pokemon in combat — they are not mindless. It builds on [[sensible-ecosystems]] and connects to [[wild-pokemon-social-hierarchy]] (leaders coordinate group behavior) and [[wild-pokemon-six-dispositions]] (intelligence informs disposition choices).
