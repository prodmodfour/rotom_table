[[Lopunny]] sheds its fur twice a year in a seasonal molt. Its winter coat grows soft, fluffy, and filled with insulating air pockets that provide substantial cold protection -- fur warm enough that humans fashion it into mufflers and hats. This biannual cycle means Lopunny's appearance and defensive qualities shift with the seasons, and it explains how the [[Buneary]] line can inhabit both temperate forests and icy mountain environments. The shed fur itself is a valuable natural resource, which could make wild Lopunny populations a point of interest for human settlements.
