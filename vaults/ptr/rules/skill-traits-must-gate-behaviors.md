A trait that modifies a skill should also serve as an [[unlock-conditions|unlock condition]] for behaviors or capabilities — not exist purely as a stat bonus. The trait should represent something the Pokemon *is* that gates things the Pokemon *can do*.

[[cute|Cute]] is the model: it gives +5 Charm, but it also gates interactions that are discretely cute — not just generically charming. The skill bonus and the unlock condition both flow from the same identity.

If a Pokemon is simply good at something without it being a gatable identity, a high base skill value is sufficient. [[Bibarel]]'s Technology Education +4 reflects dam-building intelligence, but no trait is needed because "being an engineer" doesn't gate discrete behaviors the way "being cute" does.
