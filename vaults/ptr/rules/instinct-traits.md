Instinct traits are compulsive behaviors driven by biological programming. A Pokemon with an instinct trait will act on it involuntarily when triggered — the trainer must make a social check (DC 10 + X, modified by the Pokemon's [[pokemon-social-skill-hierarchy|social skill hierarchy]]) to suppress the behavior for 1 hour. On failure, the check cannot be retried for 1 hour.

All instinct traits share this structure. What varies is the trigger and the behavior:

| Trait | Trigger | Behavior |
| ----- | ------- | -------- |
| [[challenger]] | Encounters a visibly stronger creature | Challenges it to combat |
| [[violent-instincts]] | Encounters a creature with unfavorable disposition | Attacks it |
| [[territorial]] | Creature enters claimed territory | Confronts and drives out the intruder |
| [[wanderer]] | Remains in one location too long | Attempts to leave and travel |
| [[kleptomaniac]] | Sees an unattended or held item | Attempts to steal it |
| [[hangry]] | Becomes hungry | Attacks everything indiscriminately |
| [[volatile]] | Experiences a sudden strong emotion | Involuntary energy discharge |
| [[commitment]] | Z turns without using Committed move | Must use the Committed move |
| [[hiding-instinct]] | Feels threatened | Compulsively hides and refuses to move or attack |
| [[parental-guardian-instinct]] | Young Pokemon in its care is threatened | Aggressively attacks the threat |

## Relationship to Bug Typing

Instinct traits are the Bug type's identity expression. Where other types have identity traits that grant abilities (Manipulation traits, Opportunist, Shell), Bug type's identity is its tendency to be governed by instinct. Bug types have more instinct traits, and at higher values, than other types — their behavior is driven by biological programming rather than intelligence or social sophistication.

This is why Bug beats Psychic and Dark: you cannot psychically dominate or deceive a creature that operates on pure instinct rather than complex thought.
