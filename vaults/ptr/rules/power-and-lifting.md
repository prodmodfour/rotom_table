Power measures physical strength and determines weight limits for lifting, carrying, and dragging.

| Power | Heavy Lifting | Staggering Limit | Drag Limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2–5 lb | 10 lb | 20 lb |
| 2 | 20–30 lb | 60 lb | 120 lb |
| 3 | 35–50 lb | 100 lb | 200 lb |
| 4 | 45–70 lb | 140 lb | 280 lb |
| 5 | 60–90 lb | 180 lb | 360 lb |
| 6 | 75–115 lb | 230 lb | 460 lb |
| 7 | 100–140 lb | 300 lb | 600 lb |
| 8 | 120–190 lb | 380 lb | 760 lb |
| 9 | 150–240 lb | 480 lb | 960 lb |
| 10 | 200–300 lb | 600 lb | 1200 lb |
| 11 | 250–375 lb | 750 lb | 1500 lb |

| 14 | 500–600 lb | 1200 lb | 2400 lb |
| 15 | 550–675 lb | 1350 lb | 2700 lb |
| 16 | 600–750 lb | 1500 lb | 3000 lb |

**Heavy Lifting:** −2 CS to Speed, −2 to Evasion and Accuracy. May move and act, but actions requiring hands may be restricted.

**Staggering:** Only 1 meter per Shift, no Standard Actions. −4 CS to Speed, −4 to Evasion and Accuracy. Requires a DC 4 [[skill-athletics|Athletics]] check each round to maintain.

**Drag:** Push or pull at 1 meter per round. Wheels or similar aids can multiply the drag limit.
