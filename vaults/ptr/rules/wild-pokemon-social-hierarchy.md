Wild Pokemon encounters should reflect social structures. Bug-types have hive structures, feline and canine Pokemon have packs, bird Pokemon have flocks, Water-types live in schools. A leader — typically a higher-level or evolved form — is usually present in any group.

This affects [[two-to-three-species-per-encounter]] (the leader may be a different evolutionary stage) and [[level-distribution-shapes-difficulty]] (the leader gets a disproportionate share of the level budget). The intelligence behind these social structures is governed by [[pokemon-intelligence-scales-with-niche]].

The [[murkrow-line-murder-hierarchy|Murkrow murder]] is a striking example: a single Honchkrow commands 100+ Murkrow through charismatic authority and ruthless enforcement. The [[zoroark-pack-protection|Zoroark pack]] demonstrates a contrasting model — the alpha protects through illusion and self-sacrifice rather than punishment.
