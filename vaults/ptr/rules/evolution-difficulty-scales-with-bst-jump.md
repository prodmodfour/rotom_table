The difficulty of an evolution condition should be proportional to the jump in [[base-stat-total|Base Stat Total]] between the pre-evolution and the evolved form. A small BST increase means the Pokemon isn't changing much — the condition can be easy. A large BST increase means the Pokemon is undergoing a dramatic transformation — the condition should demand more.

This is independent of stage count. A two-stage line with a massive BST jump (like [[Shroomish]] → [[Breloom]], from 29 to 48) should have a harder condition than a two-stage line with a modest jump. Similarly, a three-stage line's first evolution might still be easy if the BST gain is small, but the second evolution almost always involves a large jump and warrants a hard condition.

BST jump is a proxy for how much the Pokemon changes. A large stat shift usually comes with new body structures, new combat roles, or new ecological niches — and the evolution condition should reflect that the Pokemon has crossed a meaningful threshold to earn those changes.
