Similar species in the same habitat compete for resources. In a dark forest, Murkrow out-competes Spearow due to better camouflage — the Spearow line would go locally extinct. If both must coexist, they need differentiation: type shifts, different [[trait-definition|traits]], changed move lists, different skill allocations, or trait changes.

This is a consequence of [[sensible-ecosystems]] and works with [[type-shifts-defy-expectations]] and [[shiny-pokemon-as-variants]] as the primary mechanisms for differentiating co-habiting species.

The [[alolan-vulpix-line-mountain-adaptation|Alolan Vulpix line]] is a canonical example — the original Vulpix migrated to Alola's snowy peaks to escape lowland competition, and over generations diverged into a permanent Ice-type regional form. The [[rattata-line-urban-adaptability|Alolan Rattata]] demonstrates the same pressure in reverse — imported Yungoos forced the Rattata population to shift to nocturnal, urban living to avoid predation.

Direct competition between apex predators of the same type creates violent territorial clashes — [[garchomp-salamence-aerial-rivalry|Garchomp and Salamence]] fight over aerial food sources, while [[corviknight-skarmory-territorial-rivalry|Corviknight and Skarmory]] clash as Steel-type armored birds occupying the same niche. Lower on the food chain, [[heracross-sap-feeding-and-docility|Heracross and Pinsir]] compete for tree sap, and [[cutiefly-line-pollinator-ecology|Cutiefly competes with Butterfree]] over nectar and pollen.

In cave ecosystems, [[sableye-carbink-predation|Sableye and Gabite compete for Carbink prey]] — both hunt living gemstones, but Gabite's superior speed usually wins the race. On the surface, [[thievul-territorial-rivalries|Thievul and Liepard]] clash as stealthy Dark-type foragers occupying the same forest niche.

Note that [[eevee-line-unstable-genetics|Eevee's evolution]] is a fundamentally different mechanism — individual-level genetic restructuring triggered by stones or bonds, not population-level divergence over generations.
