Encounters should feature 2–3 different species. An encounter made entirely of one species is monotonous, and too many species is hard to manage. Companion species should supplement the main species — it makes ecological sense for Pokemon to travel in mixed groups where species complement each other.

This pairs with [[wild-pokemon-social-hierarchy]] (one species often leads the group) and [[niche-competition-drives-adaptation]] (co-existing species fill different roles).
