Fossils are calcified imprints of ancient Pokemon that retain enough DNA for revival. Rock, Water, Bug, and Grass types are most commonly fossilized.

Finding fossils requires at least a [[skill-pokemon-education|Pokemon Education]] or [[skill-survival|Survival]] check at a viable site. Difficulty scales with preservation quality:

- **Easy (DC 15):** undisturbed dried lakebeds, tar pits — intact and well-preserved
- **Moderate (DC 18):** shale cliffs, public fossil quarries — easy finds already taken
- **Hard (DC 25):** heavily disturbed areas, seismic zones — may need to reassemble fragments

Not every area has recoverable fossils. Those without the right skills can still find shards, evolutionary stones, or other items at fossil sites.

Revival requires a Reanimation Machine. The resulting egg hatches at level 2 (unlike normal eggs). Gender, traits, and moves are determined by the GM. The revived Pokemon has a chance to be shiny per [[shiny-on-one-or-hundred]].
