[[commitment|Commitment]] is the Fighting type's identity trait. Every Fighting type is born with total dedication to mastering a single technique — a specific damaging move, randomly determined, that the Pokemon is driven to perfect throughout its life.

This captures the essence of Fighting: not elemental control, not trickery, not brute force, but **disciplined dedication to martial mastery**. A Fighting type doesn't fight with everything it has — it fights with the *one thing* it has committed its entire being to perfecting. Close Combat, Mach Punch, Aura Sphere — whatever the individual's committed move, that is the technique it lives for.

The compulsion component ties Commitment into the [[instinct-traits|instinct trait]] family. A Fighting type *must* use its Committed move — it cannot resist the drive to execute its perfected technique. Training [[restraint|Restraint]] teaches the fighter patience: wait for the opening, choose the moment, rather than throwing the technique at every opportunity.

The type chart supports this:
- **Fighting > Dark**: disciplined commitment cannot be deceived or misdirected
- **Fighting > Normal**: trained technique overwhelms mundane physicality
- **Fighting > Steel/Rock/Ice**: a perfected strike finds the weak point in any defense
- **Psychic > Fighting**: mental domination exploits the predictability of a committed fighter
- **Flying > Fighting**: commitment means nothing if you can't reach the target
- **Fairy > Fighting**: no amount of martial dedication overcomes the supernatural
