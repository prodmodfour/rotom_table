[[Buneary]]'s ear positioning communicates its physiological and emotional state. When alert or sensing danger, both ears perk up straight. When one ear is coiled, Buneary is in its normal resting state with one [[buneary-ear-coil-mechanism|spring-loaded weapon]] at the ready. When both ears are rolled inward, it signals that the Buneary is unhealthy or in distress. This visible body language makes Buneary's condition easy to read for trainers and wild companions alike -- a useful diagnostic in a tabletop context where a GM might describe ear positioning to signal a wild Buneary's disposition before combat begins.
